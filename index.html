What is an election agent?

An election agent is the person legally responsible for managing a candidate's campaign during an election. Every candidate must appoint an agent, and that person is accountable for ensuring the campaign follows electoral law.

The election agent handles key administrative duties such as submitting official paperwork, managing campaign spending and donations, and ensuring all reporting requirements are met. In many cases, they also act as the main point of contact between the candidate and the local returning officer.

While candidates often appear publicly during campaigns, the election agent is the person who carries the legal responsibility for how the campaign is run behind the scenes. If rules are breached, it is typically the agent who is held accountable rather than the candidate alone.

Election agents must comply with strict spending limits and reporting deadlines set by the Electoral Commission. After the election, they are required to submit detailed expense returns that are published for public transparency.

About This Dashboard

This dashboard is built by Plymouth Political Watch as an independent election tracking tool designed to make local political information easier to access and understand.

Instead of digging through long council PDFs or scattered notices, this platform presents candidates, wards, and election data in a clear, structured, and searchable format. It is built for speed, clarity, and transparency — giving the public a faster way to see what is happening in local elections as information is released.

This is an independent project and is not affiliated with any party or council. Data is sourced from official publications where available, but no guarantee is made regarding accuracy or completeness.
